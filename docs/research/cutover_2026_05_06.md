## Why bimodal-aware buckets, not standard percentiles

The original cutover spec called for top-1% / top-5% percentile cutoffs on calibrated probabilities. At the +24h calibrated-shadow window's first daemon rebuild, that spec collapsed:

- Calibrated distribution had **8.6% of predictions piled on a single value** (the isotonic training set's highest-rate bin, mathematically `6/53 = 0.1132`)
- Both p95 and p99 percentiles locked onto this ceiling
- HIGH and MED tiers became indistinguishable

The data was telling us something honest: isotonic regression on a 14-hour training window produces step-function output with cliffs at training-bin rate-points. Not a bug — well-documented behavior on small training sets. The math was correct; the distribution shape was what the data supported.

The revised spec respects the bimodal structure:
- **HIGH** = calibrated > ceiling_value → above-ceiling outliers, "model unusually confident beyond what training showed" (~5/week, rare event signal)
- **MED** = calibrated == ceiling_value AND raw_GBM ≥ 97th percentile of raw scores → at-ceiling top-rank, "strongest signal among the at-ceiling cluster, even though calibration can't distinguish further at this training resolution" (~10/day)
- **LOW** = otherwise

When training data accumulates enough to smooth the upper tail (no value clears 1% mass), the daemon automatically falls back to standard percentile cutoffs. The bimodal-cliff workaround sunsets when the data outgrows it. This is a self-deprecating spec — captures a known successor state without requiring a future code change to reach it.

Full diagnosis + revised-spec rationale: [bucket_cutoffs_bimodal_finding.md](bucket_cutoffs_bimodal_finding.md), committed pre-cutover.

## Honest framing of the alert experience

Previous alert system: absolute threshold `predicted_prob ≥ 0.70`. Some 6h windows fired 3-7 alerts; others fired zero. The deployed k-NN was honestly calibrated to live base rates, so its score scale rarely supported 0.70-confidence calls — alerts went silent for hours at a time depending on whether any individual mint happened to land above the saturation point.

New alert system: bucket-based. **HIGH** fires on the rare-event "model unusually confident" signal (~5/week). **MED** fires on the strongest signal in the at-ceiling cluster (~10/day). Volumes self-correct against distribution drift via 24-hour cutoff rebuilds. The product behavior is consistent rather than threshold-dependent.

Trade-off: the magnitude information is preserved (calibrated probability shown alongside the bucket badge with the live base rate as comparator) but the user-visible decision surface is the bucket label, not the absolute number. This matches what the model actually does — rank predictions relative to a live base rate of ~5%.

## Decision rule — applied at +7 days post-cutover

| Branch | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Clean | HIGH 0.3-3/day rolling 7d AND MED 1-30/day; engagement steady-or-better | Cutover holds. Re-evaluate spec at T+30d for simplification path. |
| HIGH-too-rare | 0 HIGH alerts across 7 consecutive days | Lower ceiling threshold from ≥1% mass → ≥0.5%. Re-pre-register before any change. |
| MED-too-noisy | MED >25/day for 3 consecutive days | Tighten raw_gbm_p_high from p97 → p99. Re-pre-register. |
| Mixed | One bucket inside spec range, the other outside | Investigate copy/template; don't roll back. Hold 7d more. |
| Regression | HIGH AND MED both fire 0 across any 24h with prediction volume ≥100 | Roll back: `fly secrets set GBM_SHADOW_ENABLED=0`. Re-investigate. |
